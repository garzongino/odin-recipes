Lasagna

A yummy piece of lasagna

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish that layers wide pasta sheets with rich tomato sauce, creamy béchamel, and a savory meat filling of beef or sausage. Each layer is topped with a blend of mozzarella and Parmesan cheeses, creating a golden, gooey crust when baked. It's a comforting and flavorful meal, perfect for gatherings and family dinners, combining tender pasta, robust sauce, and cheesy goodness in every bite.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Tomato sauce
  • Ground beef or Italian sausage
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Egg
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Prepare the Meat Sauce:
    1. In a large skillet or saucepan, cook the ground beef or Italian sausage over medium heat until browned and cooked through. Break it up into crumbles as it cooks.
    2. Drain excess fat if necessary.
    3. Add the tomato sauce to the cooked meat. Season with salt, pepper, and any additional herbs or spices (like oregano or basil). Let it simmer for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally. Set aside.
  2. Cook the Lasagna Noodles:
    1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
    2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions until al dente.
    3. Drain the noodles and lay them flat on a baking sheet or clean surface to prevent sticking.
  3. Prepare the Ricotta Mixture:
    1. In a mixing bowl, combine the ricotta cheese with the egg. Mix well until smooth.
    2. Season the ricotta mixture with salt, pepper, and any desired herbs (like parsley).
  4. Assemble the Lasagna:
    1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
    2. In a baking dish (typically 9x13 inches), spread a thin layer of the meat sauce on the bottom.
    3. Layer lasagna noodles over the sauce, slightly overlapping them.
    4. Spread half of the ricotta mixture evenly over the noodles.
    5. Sprinkle a layer of mozzarella cheese and Parmesan cheese over the ricotta mixture.
    6. Repeat with another layer of meat sauce, noodles, ricotta mixture, and cheeses.
    7. Finish with a final layer of noodles topped with the remaining meat sauce and a generous amount of mozzarella and Parmesan cheese on top.
  5. Bake the Lasagna:
    1. Cover the baking dish loosely with aluminum foil.
    2. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes.
    3. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly and the lasagna is heated through.
  6. Let it Rest and Serve:
    1. Remove the lasagna from the oven and let it rest for about 10 minutes before slicing and serving.
    2. Garnish with fresh basil or parsley if desired.
    3. Enjoy your homemade lasagna!
